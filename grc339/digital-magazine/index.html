Medicinal plants in your own backyard

Native Californians have cultivated a wide variety of plants for medicial purposes, making careful use of leaves, flowers, stems, and roots to treat ailments ranging from rash to indigestion. Pictured is the Shrubby Monkeyflower, its leaves used by the Coast Miwok and Pomo to treat sores and burns and its roots to treat fever, dysentery, and diarrhea.

Sacred species: A look into spiritually signficant plants

A number of plant species are significant to cultural and spiritual rituals, particularly for Native Californians. White sage is burned for meditation, smudging and cleansing of spirit and dwelling. In some beliefs, white sage smoke is believed to provide protection from negative spirits.

Its bark is better than its bite: 5 tree barks with medicial benefits
