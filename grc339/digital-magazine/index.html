Five Traditional Californian Plant Medicines and How They Might Work

Native Californians have cultivated a wide variety of plants for medicial purposes, making careful use of leaves, flowers, stems, and roots to treat ailments ranging from rash to indigestion. Pictured is the Shrubby Monkeyflower, its leaves used by the Coast Miwok and Pomo to treat sores and burns and its roots to treat fever, dysentery, and diarrhea.

Its bark is better than its bite

A tree's bark is often overlooked, and its impressive healing qualities along with it. Mountain Mahogony's bark is antihaemorrhagic, cardiac, stomachic and tonic. Its decoction has been used in the treatment of coughs and colds, pneumonia, spitting up of blood, and much more!
